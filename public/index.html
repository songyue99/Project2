ü•≤ Many dialects are dying in China

Many places in the world, including China, are witnessing the rapid disappearance of dialects - along with the disappearance rich cultures behind them.

Dialects fade away due to many reasons: the formalization of one language (or only a few) in a nation, the need to communicate in an easy and mutually-understandable language throughout globalization, and the widespread use of technology (which is heavily based on the one single standard language). Dialects carry not only the linguistic value of diversity but also the rich cultures in the local areas. It's extremely difficult to describe or document local cultural events or rituals without dialects. People also tend to cherish a lot the memories buried in them!

ü§Ø So how might we save the dialects?

One of the effective ways to pass along dialects is to use them online: on social media, give it meanings and make sound documentations.

There was a successful online campaign to revive a Danish dialect in 2015, which used a combination of sound annotations and memes. It demonstrated the great power that technology can play in saving dialects in the modern world, instead of destroying them.

Cantonese speakers also successfully invented sounds annotations to talk to each other online even though it is only recognized as an official spoken language in Hongkong, Macau, and the Guangzhou Province.

For my Connections Lab final project @IMA Low Res, I made this meme-guessing game, which utilizes dialect pinyin(a way of denoting sounds and tones with English characters) as the main connection method. Through the fun process, I wish to remind people of their intimate relationship with dialects and encourage them to use dialect more online!

üåÆ How to play the game?

Form a group of no less than 2 people, pick 1 person as the describer, the rest people will be the guessors. Make sure everyone's on the game page before starting the game!

Describer

Hi describer! Once you are on the game page, choose a meme buzzword, and describe it in your dialect. If any guessor guesses correctly, tell them it's right!

Guesser

Hi guessors! On the game page, click "I'm guessing" and you'll see a list of memes that you are guessing from. Feel free to ask the describer more questions about the meme! The describer would let you know if anyone guesses right!
